A compilation of books you must read before you die

by Rheaa Rao

Books let you travel the world without a passport. They make staying in on snow days worth it. Here's a compilation of a few of the best ones.

Let's start with a list of authors. Feel free to add some more.

  • Haruki Murakami
  • Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie
  • Anuja Chauhan
  • Joanne Harris

Fun facts

  • Murakami didn't start out as a writer...but as a bar owner. He also writes about running
  • Adichie gave a commencement speech at Wellesley in 2015.

But if I had to choose between their work. Here's my list:

  1. Hard boiled wonder and the end of the world

    2. This is a absurd fantasy novel that boggles the mind.
    One of Murakami's best books.

  2. Battle for Bittora

    3. Popcorn galore! The perfect romance novel.
    It is instrinsically Indian in it's humor without
    subscribing to Indian cliches. Zain Altaf Khan is
    the ultimate hero.

  3. Americanah

    4. A novel about immigrants growing into themselves
    in an alien culture. A love story, a coming of age
    story, but without the cliches. And yes, highly relatable.

  4. Five quarters of the Orange

    5. Joanne Harris is famous for her famous novel Chocolat.
    But this one is a hidden treasure. Recipes, old childhood
    memories, written in a very Arundati Roy style.
